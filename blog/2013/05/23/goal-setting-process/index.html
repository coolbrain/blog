Goal-Setting Process

May 23rd, 2013

This passage is talking about Goal-Setting Process.

1.Decide exactly what you want in every key area of your life. Start off by idealing.

2.write it down. They must be clear, specific, detailed and measurable.

3.set a deadline. Your subsconscious mind uses deadlines as “forcing systems ” to drive you, consciously and unconsciously toward achieving your goal on scheduler.

4.Identity the obstacles that you will have to overcome to achieve your goal. Only 20% of the reasons you are not achieving your goal are on the outside. Always start with yourself.The 80/20 Rule applied to constraints.

5.Identity the knowledge, information and skills you will need to achieve your goal. Especially, identify the skills that you will have to develop to be in the top 10% of your field.

6.Identity the people whose help and cooperation you will acquire to achieve your goal. One key person at a certain time and place in your life will make all the difference.

7.Make a list of everything you will have to do to achieve your goal. ” A journey of a thousand miles begin with a single step”. You can build the biggest wall in the world one brick at a time.

8.Organize your list into a plan. You organize this list by arranging the steps that you have identified by sequence and priority. The 80/20 Rule says that 80% of your result will come from 20% of your activities.

9.Make a plan. Organize your list into a series of steps from the beginning all the way through to the completion of your goal.

10.Select your number one, most important task for each day.Set priorities on your list using the 80/20 rule.

11.Develop the habit of self-discipline. Once you have decide on your most important task. Resolve to concentrate singled-mindedly on that one task until it is 100% completed.

12.Practise visualization on your goals. Create clear, vivid, exciting, emotional picture of your goals as if they were already a reality.

Motto: No man’s education is ever finished. A man’s reading program should be as carefully planned as his daily diet, for that too is food, without which he cannot mentally develop.
